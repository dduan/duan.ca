Taking Screenshots On Linux

Daniel Duan · 2020-06-18

Having used macOS for a number of years, I've formed some habit that I'm never going to shake. Among them is taking screenshots with Alt-Shift-3 and Alt-Shift-4, follow up with area or window selection. So, when I bought a PC and put Linux on it, it's time to mess with it for these habits!

And, it didn't take too long to solve. I've found maim. Writing this down for mine and maybe, yours, future reference.

To capture the entire screen:

bash -c "maim ~/Screenshots/fullscreen_$(date +%s).png"

To active area/windows selection for the capture:

bash -c "maim -s ~/Screenshots/area_$(date +%s).png"
